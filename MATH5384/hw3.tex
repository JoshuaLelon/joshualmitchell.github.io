\textbf{Let A be a nonempty set, and let}
\eqn{S_A = \bk{f : A \lra A : \textrm{f is both 1 to 1 and onto}}}
\textbf{Show that \uw{S}{A} is a group under composition. Is \uw{S}{A} an Abelian group?}

\balist
\item \textbf{Closure:} Want to show that, \fa f, g \mem \uw{S}{A}, f o g \mem \uw{S}{A}

Let f, g \mem \uw{S}{A}, and let a \mem A.

Since both f and g are well defined, f(a) and g(a) exist.

Since both f and g map to A, f(a) \mem A and g(a) \mem A. \bpth{1}

Since both f and g are one to one, f(a) and g(a) are unique. \bpth{2}

By \bpth{1} and \bpth{2}, f(g(a)) and g(f(a)) both exist and are unique.

Therefore, both f o g and g o f are one-to-one.

Now, we want to show that they're onto. 

Suppose \exs \uw{a}{0} \mem A such that f(g(a)) $\neq$ \uw{a}{0} (or that g(f(a)) $\neq$ \uw{a}{0}), \fa a \mem A.

However, if \uw{a}{0} \mem A, then it gets mapped onto by both f and g.

So that means there exists some \uw{a}{f} and \uw{a}{g} in A such that f(\uw{a}{g}) \eql \uw{a}{0} (or g(\uw{a}{f}) \eql \uw{a}{0}).

And since \uw{a}{f} and \uw{a}{g} are in A, they get mapped to by f and g, respectively.

Thus, a contradiction.

\item \textbf{Associativity:} Want to show that, \fa f, g, h \mem \uw{S}{A}, (f o g) o h \eql f o (g o h).

Let f, g, h \mem \uw{S}{A}, and let a \mem A.

Let h(a) \eql \uw{a}{h}, g(h(a)) \eql \uw{a}{gh}, f(a) \eql \uw{a}{f}, f(g(a)) \eql \uw{a}{fg}, which are all defined since f, g, and h are all well defined and onto.

Notice that ((f o g) o h)(a) \eql f(g(\uw{a}{h})) and (f o (g o h))(a) \eql f(\uw{a}{gh}).

Want to show: g(\uw{a}{h}) \eql \uw{a}{gh}.

Well, g(\uw{a}{h}) \eql g(a(h)) by definition, and \uw{a}{gh} \eql g(a(h)) by definition.

Hence, result.

\item \textbf{Identity:} Want to show that \exs I \mem \uw{S}{A} such that I o f \eql f o I \eql f, \fa f \mem \uw{S}{A}.



\item \textbf{Inverse:} Want to show that, \fa f \mem \uw{S}{A}, \exs \uf{f}{-1} such that f(\uf{f}{-1}(a)) \eql \uf{f}{-1}(f(a)) \eql a, \fa a \mem A.



\elist

\uw{S}{A} is \textbf{NOT} an Abelian group (since function composition is not commutative).
