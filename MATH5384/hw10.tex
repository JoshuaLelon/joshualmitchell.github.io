Let H \eql \bk{(1), (12)(34)} in \uw{A}{4}.

\balist
\item Show that H is not normal in \uw{A}{4}.

Well, recall that a subgroup H of G is normal iff gH \eql Hg \fa g \mem G.

So all we need to do is find a g \mem \uw{A}{4} such that gH $\neq$ Hg.

Notice: (23) \mem \uw{A}{4}.

(23)H \eql \bk{(23)(1), (23)(12)(34)} \eql \bk{(23), (1342)}

H(23) \eql \bk{(1)(23), (12)(34)(23)} \eql \bk{(23), (1243)}

\bk{(23), (1342)} $\neq$ \bk{(23), (1243)}

Thus, H is not normal in \uw{A}{4}

\item Referring to the multiplication table for \uw{A}{4} in Table 5.1 on page 105, show that, although \uw{\afa}{6}H \eql \uw{\afa}{7}H and \uw{\afa}{9}H \eql \uw{\afa}{11}H, it is not true that \uw{\afa}{6}\uw{\afa}{9}H \eql \uw{\afa}{7}\uw{\afa}{11}H.

\uw{\afa}{6} \eql (243), \uw{\afa}{7} \eql (142), \uw{\afa}{9} \eql (132), and \uw{\afa}{11} \eql (234)

So, let's look at both:

\uw{\afa}{6}\uw{\afa}{9}H \lla ? \lra \uw{\afa}{7}\uw{\afa}{11}H

(243)(132)H \lla ? \lra (142)(234)H

\bk{(243)(132)(1), (243)(132)(12)(34)} \lla ? \lra \bk{(142)(234)(1), (142)(234)(12)(34)}

\bk{(12)(34), (1)} \lla ? \lra \bk{(14)(23),(13)(24)}

Nope! Those sets are not equal, so it's not true that \uw{\afa}{6}\uw{\afa}{9}H \eql \uw{\afa}{7}\uw{\afa}{11}H.
