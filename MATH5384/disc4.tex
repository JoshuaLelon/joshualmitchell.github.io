\ssc{Page 24 Exercise 11}{

\textbf{Let n and a be positive integers and let d \eql gcd(a, n). Show that the equation ax \eqw 1 mod n has a solution iff d \eql 1. (This exercise is referred to in Chapter 2.)}

Let a, n \mem \uf{\bz}{+}.

Let d \eql gcd(a, n)

\lra

Want to show: ax \eqw 1 mod n \rar d \eql 1

Suppose ax \eqw 1 mod n.

Then \exs t \mem \bz st 

tn \eql 1 \ms ax

tn \ps xa \eql 1

Since \exs t, x \mem \bz such that tn \ps xa \eql 1,

a and n are relatively prime.

Therefore, gcd(a, n) \eql d \eql 1.

\lla

Want to show: d \eql 1 \rar ax \eqw 1 mod n

Suppose d \eql 1.

Then gcd(a, n) \eql 1.

Thus, \exs t, x \mem \bz such that tn \ps ax \eql 1.

tn \ps ax \eql 1

tn \eql 1 \ms ax

Thus, t is a possible solution to ax \eqw 1 mod n

}
